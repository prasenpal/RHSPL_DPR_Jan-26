Section 03

Project Details

Comprehensive capacity enhancement with advanced infrastructure

Project Background

The proposed hospital capacity enhancement project addresses growing demand for quality secondary and tertiary healthcare services in Bankura and surrounding catchment areas.

Bankura district faces significant gap in access to advanced diagnostics, critical care, and specialized surgical facilities, compelling patients to travel to Durgapur or Kolkata.

The project aims to bridge this infrastructure gap by expanding bed capacity, strengthening critical care and neonatal services, and introducing high-end diagnostic and surgical capabilities.

Key Components

Construction covering foundation to plinth, superstructure up to 5th floor, hospital-grade finishing including lifts, fire-fighting, MGPS, HVAC, electrical, plumbing, CCTV, and modular OT infrastructure.
Expansion of inpatient capacity with general wards, ICU beds, NICU facilities, dialysis units, and fully equipped nursing stations.
Installation of 1.5 Tesla MRI, Cath Lab, Digital X-Ray, Colour Doppler, pathology automation systems, and critical care monitoring devices.
Multiple specialized OTs including Orthopedic OT, CathLab and General Surgery OT with modern anesthesia workstations, laparoscopy, and surgical diathermy systems.

Section 07

Risk Evaluation & Mitigants

Comprehensive risk assessment with appropriate mitigation strategies

Promoter & Management Risk Low

Promoters possess prior experience in business and civil construction. Demonstrated ability to generate profits. Risk mitigated by engaging experienced hospital administrators and professional management.

Market / Competition Risk Moderate

Healthcare sector in Bankura comprises government hospitals and private nursing homes. Demand-supply gap for ICU beds, diagnostics, and specialty services supports sustained demand.

Demand Risk Low-Moderate

Healthcare services are non-discretionary. Government insurance schemes improve affordability and utilization. Bankura serves as referral hub for surrounding areas.

Input Risk Moderate

Key inputs include qualified doctors, nursing staff, medicines, consumables. Mitigated through phased expansion, multiple supplier tie-ups, and buffer inventories.

Operational Risk Moderate

Complex processes including patient safety, infection control, equipment uptime. Mitigated through SOPs, preventive maintenance, staff training, and hospital information systems.

Regulatory Risk Moderate

Subject to clinical establishment norms, fire safety, pharmacy licensing, biomedical waste management. Mitigated through statutory approvals and compliance audits.

Funding Risk Low-Moderate

Project funded through balanced 80:20 debt-equity structure. Promoter group is resourceful to bring required equity. Phased expansion limits upfront capital stress.

Policy / Govt Scheme Risk Moderate

Portion of revenues expected from government health insurance schemes. Mitigated through diversification of payer mix and conservative cash-flow planning.

Overall Risk Profile Assessment

Considering the essential nature of healthcare services, favorable demand-supply dynamics in Bankura, and phased expansion strategy, the overall risk profile is assessed as MODERATE, which is acceptable for a healthcare infrastructure project with long-term sustainability.
